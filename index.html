The Tools

Every module your shop actually needs

Start with one module. Add more as you see value. Each tool is built specifically for job shop workflows — not adapted from generic business software.

Most Requested

Digital Production Board

Replace the whiteboard. Drag-and-drop jobs between machines and shifts. See every job's status, operator, and due date in real time. Flags late jobs automatically. Sales, production, and shipping all see the same board.

  • Drag-and-drop job scheduling per machine
  • Auto-flags jobs at risk of missing due date
  • Shared view for all departments
  • Syncs with work orders
Shops report 10–15% throughput improvement from better schedule visibility

Job Costing Feedback Loop

Know which jobs made money and which didn't. Operators tap start/stop on a tablet or scan a QR code. Actual labor cost per operation vs. your quote — automatically. Stops margin leaks before they become a pattern.

  • QR code or tablet tap to clock in/out per job
  • Actual vs. estimated cost per operation
  • Dashboard: which jobs lost money and why
  • Feeds back into future quote accuracy
Average shop finds 8–12% margin gap between quoted and actual on first review

Digital Shop Traveler

Kill the paper bundle. Each job gets a QR code. Operators scan to see drawings, step instructions, and tolerances. Tap through each operation. Record actual dimensions. Flag issues with a photo. Auto-archives when the job ships.

  • QR code per job, works on any tablet or phone
  • Step-by-step instructions with drawings attached
  • In-process dimension recording
  • Photo attachment for non-conformances
  • Full audit trail, auto-archived at ship
Eliminates reprints, re-entry, and lost travelers — saves 45+ min/day per operator

Customer Status Portal

Stop answering "where's my order?" calls. Every customer gets a link to track their jobs in real time — quote received, in production, % complete, shipped with tracking. Proactive alerts when status changes. No more going dark during production.

  • Customer-facing order tracking link per job
  • Auto email/SMS at each status change
  • Proactive alerts when a job goes at-risk
  • No customer login required
Eliminates 1–2 hrs/day of inbound status calls for the average shop

Preventive Maintenance Tracker

Set PM schedules per machine by spindle hours, cycle count, or calendar. Get alerts before something breaks, not after. Log every service — what was done, who did it, what parts were replaced. Track downtime causes. Nothing more complicated than it needs to be.

  • PM schedules by hours, cycles, or calendar
  • Alerts before due — not when overdue
  • Service log per machine
  • Downtime cause tracking
  • No CMMS complexity — built for machinists
Preventing one major spindle repair = $5K–$15K saved. PM tracking pays for itself in month one.

Material & Remnant Inventory

Know what stock you have before you order more. Track bar stock, sheet, tube, and plate — including remnants and drops. Before quoting a job, check if you already have the material. Alerts when minimum stock is hit. Works offline on a shop tablet.

  • Track raw stock and remnants by size and material
  • Check available stock before buying
  • Low-stock alerts with reorder suggestions
  • Ties to work orders — deducts material on job start
Shops reduce material overbuying by 10–15% — saving $10K–$20K/year on a $200K material budget

Certificate of Conformance Generator

Job ships — CoC is automatically generated pre-filled with PO number, part number, material heat numbers, revision, and inspector sign-off. One click to email to the customer. Material certs attached at receiving and linked to jobs. No more Word documents.

  • Auto-generated CoC at job close
  • One-click email to customer
  • Material cert attached at receiving, linked to job
  • Audit-ready document storage
  • AS9100 / IATF compatible format
Saves 30–60 min per shipment. For 20 jobs/week, that's 10–20 hrs/week recovered.

Outside Service Tracker

When a job goes to heat treat, anodize, plating, or waterjet — it shouldn't disappear. Log where it went and when it's due back. Automated follow-up emails to vendors at set intervals. Dashboard of every job currently out and what's overdue.

  • Log jobs sent out with vendor, PO, and due date
  • Auto follow-up emails to vendors
  • Dashboard: what's out, where, and overdue
  • Confirm receipt back and update job status
"Jobs sit at vendors for weeks past due and nobody notices until the customer calls."

FAQ

Common questions

How is this different from JobBOSS², ProShop, or our current ERP?

Those systems try to do everything — which means they're complex, expensive per user ($150–$500/month per seat), and take months to implement. Most shops never use more than 30% of their ERP's features. Axon is modular — you pick the one or two problems costing you the most right now, and those tools are purpose-built for exactly that. You're running on day one, not month six.

Do my operators need training?

Less than 10 minutes per module. Operators interact with a single-screen tablet interface — tap to start a job, tap to complete a step, scan a QR code. We designed every action to take under 5 seconds or operators won't do it consistently. The dashboard for managers is similarly direct.

Does it work without internet? Our shop floor connection is unreliable.

Yes. All operator-facing modules work offline and sync when connection is restored. Shop floor tablets can lose internet for hours without losing any data. We built this specifically for the manufacturing environment.

Will it connect to QuickBooks or our ERP?

QuickBooks integration is included in all plans. ERP connectors (JobBOSS², E2, Epicor, ProShop) are available on the Enterprise plan. Most small shops don't need deep integration — the modules work standalone and the data export is compatible with any accounting system.

What's the 30-day ROI guarantee?

Every module has a measurable outcome. We agree on the metric before you start — time saved, jobs costing feedback recovered, downtime prevented. If you can't point to clear ROI at day 30, we refund month one. No paperwork, no negotiation.

Can I start with just one module and add more later?

Yes — that's the entire design philosophy. Most shops start with the Production Board or Job Costing because those have the most immediate visible impact. Once the team is comfortable, they add a second module. You never have to "go live" on a big system all at once.
